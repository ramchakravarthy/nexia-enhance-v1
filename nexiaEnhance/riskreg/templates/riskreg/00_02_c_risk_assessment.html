Tailoring

The risk register must be tailored to the specific circumstances of the firm. It is not possible to conceive of all the possible factors affecting a firm and thus it does not provide a definitive list of all the quality objectives, quality risks and potential responses that may exist, and additional items may need to be added in order to ensure that the risk assessment process is effective in ensuring that responses are implemented that address all significant quality risks that may exist.

There are 6 categories of quality objectives and a number of objectives, risks and risk responses identified by ISQM 1. Additional categories may be created for further quality objectives that are identified by the firm, although this is expected to be rare.

Firms who have filled out their quality management system using the excel spreadsheet provided on the Nexia website can import their excel files into this platform using the "Import risk register" option available under the "Risk register" option to the left.

Firms can add new risks by clicking on the "Create entry" option under the "Risk register" option on the left. There are five types of entries that can be created:

1. Risk library entry

This it an entry from the risk library. Following the top-down approach, you can create a risk from the risk library. Whilst filling out the risk, there are a number of associated attributes that should be filled out. You can fill some of these as you create a risk and revisit them later to fill out the other details.

Once created, the risk and risk response can be edited but the quality objective category and quality objective cannot be edited, as per the instructions set out in the ISQM system instructions found here.

2. Custom risk response

This is where a user will create a custom/tailored risk response for a quality risk that has been identified in the risk library.
Unlike the risk library entry, the quality objective category and quality objective can be edited in addition to the quality risk and risk response of these entries.

3. Custom risk and risk response

This is where a user will create a custom/tailored risk and risk response for a quality objective that has been identified in the risk library.
The entry can be fully edited.

4. Custom objective, risk and risk response

This is where a user will create a custom/tailored quality objective, quality risk and risk response for a quality objective category that has been identified in the risk library.
The entry can be fully edited.

5. Custom category, objective, risk and risk response

This is where a user will create a custom/tailored quality objective category, quality objective, quality risk and risk response.
The entry can be fully edited.


Note: All risks should be signed off by the owner and the reviewer.

ISQM 1 and certain other standards do though set out a number of mandatory responses that should be implemented by all firms unless they are deemed irrelevant to their practice. Care should be taken before any of these responses are amended or deleted in order to ensure that firms comply with the requirements of the regulatory framework.

Users of the Mercia audit methodology will find that it contains a number of forms and procedures that will assist with completion of these responses, and these are highlighted here, as are other ways in which Mercia can assist with implementing your system of quality management.
